Wellbutrin vs Lexapro: Differences in How They Treat Depression

Two of the most prescribed antidepressants work in completely different ways. Understanding how Wellbutrin (bupropion) and Lexapro (escitalopram) differ can help you and your doctor choose the right one.

Medically reviewed by Dr. Agustin Arrieta, MD
Last updated: February 2026 · 10 min read

Wellbutrin and Lexapro are both effective, widely prescribed antidepressants — but they work through entirely different mechanisms. This isn't just a pharmacological detail. It translates into meaningful differences in side effects, who they work best for, and how they feel day to day.

If you're trying to decide between them, or wondering whether to switch, here's what the evidence says.

Mechanism

How They Work Differently

Lexapro (Escitalopram): The Serotonin Path

Lexapro is a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI). It works by blocking the reabsorption (reuptake) of serotonin in the brain, making more of this neurotransmitter available to nerve cells. Serotonin plays a key role in mood regulation, sleep, appetite, and anxiety.

Lexapro is considered the most selective SSRI, meaning it acts almost exclusively on serotonin transporters with minimal effects on other neurotransmitter systems. This selectivity contributes to its relatively clean side effect profile compared to older SSRIs.

Wellbutrin (Bupropion): The Dopamine-Norepinephrine Path

Wellbutrin is a norepinephrine-dopamine reuptake inhibitor (NDRI) — the only antidepressant in its class. It increases the activity of both norepinephrine (involved in alertness and energy) and dopamine (involved in motivation, pleasure, and reward).

Because Wellbutrin doesn't affect serotonin, it avoids the serotonin-related side effects that are common with SSRIs — most notably sexual dysfunction and weight gain. Its dopamine activity is also why it's FDA-approved for smoking cessation (marketed as Zyban).

Side Effects

Side Effect Comparison

Sexual Side Effects

This is the most significant clinical difference between the two medications. SSRIs like Lexapro are well known for causing sexual dysfunction — including decreased libido, difficulty achieving orgasm, and erectile dysfunction. Studies report rates of 30-40% with escitalopram.

Wellbutrin, by contrast, has sexual side effect rates comparable to placebo (2-5%). In fact, bupropion is sometimes added to an SSRI specifically to counteract sexual side effects. A study published in the Journal of Clinical Psychiatry found that adding bupropion to an SSRI significantly improved sexual functioning in patients experiencing SSRI-induced sexual dysfunction.

Weight Changes

Lexapro can cause modest weight gain, typically 2-5 pounds, though some patients gain more with long-term use. The mechanism likely involves serotonin's effects on appetite regulation.

Wellbutrin is weight-neutral or may cause slight weight loss. A study comparing bupropion to SSRIs over 12 months found that bupropion patients lost an average of 2.4 pounds while SSRI patients gained an average of 1.5 pounds.

Other Common Side Effects

Wellbutrin Common Side Effects

  • Dry mouth (17-26%)
  • Insomnia (11-20%)
  • Headache (25-34%)
  • Nausea (13%)
  • Agitation or restlessness
  • Seizure risk at high doses (dose-dependent, ~0.4% at 450 mg/day)

Lexapro Common Side Effects

  • Nausea (15%)
  • Drowsiness (6-13%)
  • Insomnia (9-12%)
  • Headache (24%)
  • Sexual dysfunction (30-40%)
  • GI disturbance (diarrhea, constipation)
  • Emotional blunting in some patients
Which Is Better For You

Choosing Between Wellbutrin and Lexapro

Lexapro may be the better choice if:

  • You have depression with co-occurring anxiety — Lexapro is FDA-approved for generalized anxiety disorder and is one of the most effective SSRIs for anxious depression
  • You have panic disorder or social anxiety — SSRIs are first-line for these conditions
  • You experience irritability and agitation as primary symptoms — Lexapro's calming effect on serotonin can help
  • You have a history of seizures — bupropion lowers the seizure threshold
  • You have an eating disorder — Wellbutrin is contraindicated in bulimia and anorexia due to seizure risk

Wellbutrin may be the better choice if:

  • You've experienced sexual side effects on SSRIs — Wellbutrin has the lowest rate of sexual dysfunction of any antidepressant
  • Weight gain is a major concern — Wellbutrin is weight-neutral
  • Your depression presents primarily as fatigue, low energy, and poor concentration — Wellbutrin's activating profile targets these symptoms
  • You also want to quit smoking — Wellbutrin is FDA-approved for smoking cessation
  • You have ADHD symptoms alongside depression — bupropion has some evidence for improving attention and concentration
  • You've experienced emotional blunting on SSRIs — Wellbutrin's dopamine activity may preserve emotional range

Can You Take Both Together?

Yes — combining Wellbutrin and Lexapro is one of the most common antidepressant combinations in clinical practice. Because they work on completely different neurotransmitter systems, they complement each other:

  • Lexapro addresses serotonin, helping with mood, anxiety, and emotional stability
  • Wellbutrin addresses dopamine and norepinephrine, adding energy, motivation, and concentration
  • Wellbutrin may offset Lexapro's sexual side effects
  • The combination may be more effective than either alone for treatment-resistant cases

A study published in the American Journal of Psychiatry as part of the STAR*D trial found that augmenting an SSRI with bupropion was one of the most well-tolerated switching/augmentation strategies for patients who didn't respond to initial SSRI monotherapy.

Important: Never combine or change antidepressants without your prescriber's guidance. Drug interactions and dosing adjustments need medical oversight.

Effectiveness: What the Research Says

In terms of raw antidepressant effectiveness, Lexapro and Wellbutrin are broadly comparable for treating major depressive disorder.

The landmark 2018 Lancet network meta-analysis of 522 trials by Cipriani et al. ranked escitalopram (Lexapro) among the most effective and best-tolerated antidepressants. Bupropion (Wellbutrin) ranked in the middle for effectiveness but was among the best tolerated.

However, "effectiveness" isn't one-dimensional. The right antidepressant is the one that treats your specific symptom profile with side effects you can live with. A medication that's slightly less effective on paper but doesn't cause sexual dysfunction or weight gain may result in better real-world outcomes because patients are more likely to stay on it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Wellbutrin vs Lexapro FAQ

What is the main difference between Wellbutrin and Lexapro?

Wellbutrin (bupropion) is an NDRI that increases norepinephrine and dopamine. Lexapro (escitalopram) is an SSRI that increases serotonin. This leads to different side effect profiles: Wellbutrin has lower risk of sexual side effects and weight gain, while Lexapro is better for co-occurring anxiety.

Which causes less weight gain, Wellbutrin or Lexapro?

Wellbutrin. It's weight-neutral and may cause slight weight loss. Lexapro can cause modest weight gain of 2-5 pounds, with some patients gaining more over long-term use.

Does Wellbutrin cause sexual side effects?

Wellbutrin has a significantly lower rate of sexual side effects (2-5%) compared to Lexapro (30-40%). It's sometimes prescribed specifically to counteract SSRI-induced sexual dysfunction.

Can you take Wellbutrin and Lexapro together?

Yes, this is one of the most common antidepressant combinations. They work on different neurotransmitter systems and complement each other. Wellbutrin can help offset Lexapro's sexual side effects while Lexapro addresses anxiety that Wellbutrin may not cover. Always combine under medical supervision.

Is Wellbutrin or Lexapro better for anxiety?

Lexapro is generally the better choice for depression with anxiety. It's FDA-approved for generalized anxiety disorder and has strong evidence for panic disorder. Wellbutrin can sometimes increase anxiety, especially early in treatment or at higher doses.

Which is better for depression with fatigue?

Wellbutrin is typically preferred. Its dopamine and norepinephrine activity provides an activating effect that targets fatigue, low energy, and poor concentration. Lexapro can cause drowsiness in some patients.
